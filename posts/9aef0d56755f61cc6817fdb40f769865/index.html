How to revising your document, based on Silyn-Roberts, ch. 16

Five stage process:

1. Structure and content only

  • Have you followed your plan?
  • Is the overall structure logical?
  • Are the sections in a logical order?
  • Are the subsections within a section in a logical order?
  • Will the relationship between ideas be clear to the reader?

2. Style, grammar, punctuation, equations

3. Proofread

Find:

  • typos
  • irregular spacing
  • errors in punctuation
  • errors in spelling
  • omitted words
  • funny fonts or formatting
  • errors in equations, figures, tables

4. Formatting

Things to consider:

  • Visual look of document: Make your document look visually storing and not boring or daunting/make sure that your make formatting decisions based on careful thought or on established guidelines. Don't be sloppy.
  • Justification
  • Margin width
  • Bullets or listing: use it not too much
  • Page breaks: don't put a heading at the bottom of a page/don't cut a table in half from bottom of one page to top of next

5. Document integrity

Avoid discrepancies such as:

  • incorrectly numbered section headings
  • incorrect figure or table numbers or references in text
  • referencing mistakes
  • incorrect Table of Contents numbers
  • missing figures/tables
